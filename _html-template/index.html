Latest Issue

Cumbia in Technicolor: Psychedelic Grooves to Ignite Your Weekend

Let the weekend bloom in swirling color as we dive into the hypnotic world of psychedelic cumbia. This isn't your abuela's dance floor—these are cosmic rhythms, electric guitars, and swirling synths that stretch tradition into new dimensions. Join us as we celebrate Latinx artists who blend ancestral beats with mind-bending sounds, inviting you to lose yourself in the kaleidoscope and start your weekend on a wave of pure, vibrant energy.

Featured Tracks

1. Close To Me - Locobeach

Locobeach's 'Close To Me' likely blends Latin rhythms with a modern sensibility, creating a sound that's both familiar and fresh. Expect a groove that makes you want to move, perfect for a chill hangout or a low-key dance party.

2. Terror/Cacts, Barzo - La Corriente (Barzo Remix)

This remix of "La Corriente" pulses with a dark, hypnotic energy, blending electronic textures with the original's Latin American roots. Barzo crafts a late-night vibe perfect for shadowy dancefloors.

3. Combo Tezeta - Ritmo Interestelar

"Ritmo Interestelar" by Combo Tezeta is a cosmic journey through sound, blending traditional Latin rhythms with futuristic, interstellar vibes. The track creates a unique sonic landscape, perfect for both dancing under the stars and contemplating the mysteries of the universe.

4. La Meseta - Candeleros

Evoking the vast landscapes of its namesake, "La Meseta" unfolds with a spacious, ambient soundscape. The track creates a meditative mood, perfect for contemplation.

5. Cumbia Trosky - Frente Cumbiero

This track pulses with a hypnotic, slightly off-kilter energy, blending traditional cumbia rhythms with a modern, almost industrial edge. It's a sound that feels both familiar and strangely futuristic, perfect for late-night explorations.

Artist Spotlight

Terror Cactus: Desert Bloom of Noise-Pop

Forget the cacti you know. Terror Cactus, hailing from the sun-baked landscapes of Arizona, isn't about prickly exteriors. Instead, they're a blooming oasis of noise-pop, injecting a healthy dose of fuzz and melody into the arid indie scene.

Their Bandcamp page is a treasure trove of lo-fi gems, each track a sun-drenched postcard from a world where distortion is a virtue and catchy hooks are king. Think early Jesus and Mary Chain meets a DIY spirit, with a dash of desert psychedelia thrown in for good measure.

Terror Cactus crafts songs that are both abrasive and strangely comforting, like finding shade under a thorny bush on a scorching day. If you're craving something raw, honest, and dripping with reverb, dive into the prickly paradise that is Terror Cactus. You might just find your new favorite sonic bloom.
